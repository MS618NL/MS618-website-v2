## SEO as a growth strategy, not a technical exercise

Most companies misunderstand SEO. They see it as a technical checklist: optimise titles, write meta descriptions, improve page speed. Those are certainly components of SEO — but they are symptoms of a larger strategy, not the strategy itself.

At MS618, we treat SEO for what it is: a growth engine. A systematic approach to attracting the right people, at the right moment, through organic search traffic. Not once, but structurally.

## Our approach

### 1. Strategic foundation: search intent and market analysis

Before we write a single word or fix a single technical issue, we understand the market. What are your ideal customers searching for? At what point in their decision-making process? With what intent?

We map the complete search landscape in your market: from top-of-funnel orientation to bottom-of-funnel purchase intent. This becomes the foundation of everything that follows.

### 2. Technical SEO audit

A strong foundation is essential. We analyse:

- Crawlability and indexation (no pages getting lost)
- Core Web Vitals (LCP, CLS, INP — directly affecting rankings)
- Site structure and internal linking (how Google navigates your site)
- Duplicate content and canonicalisation
- Structured data / Schema.org implementation
- Mobile-first indexing

### 3. Content architecture

Based on the search landscape, we design the ideal content architecture for your site. Which pages do you need? How do they relate to each other? Which search terms are a priority?

We work with a pillar-cluster model: strong, comprehensive pillar pages for each main topic, supported by targeted cluster content that answers specific questions.

### 4. Content creation and optimisation

Content that ranks is content that fully addresses search intent. We write and optimise:

- Service pages that build trust and convert
- Blog and knowledge articles that establish authority
- Landing pages for specific audience segments
- Case studies that provide social proof

### 5. Link building and authority development

Links remain one of the strongest ranking factors. But not all links are equal. We focus on high-quality, thematically relevant links through:

- Thought leadership placements in trade publications
- Digital PR and newsjacking
- Partnerships and co-creation
- Competitor analysis and link gap strategy
