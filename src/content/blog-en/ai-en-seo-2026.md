## The panic is understandable — but misplaced

Every week brings new articles claiming AI signals the death of SEO. Google SGE is stealing clicks. ChatGPT is replacing search engines. Perplexity is eating market share.

There's one thing all these narratives agree on: everything is changing.

But that's nothing new. SEO has faced fundamental shifts for decades — from keywords to intent, from links to E-E-A-T, from desktop to mobile. And every time, people declared SEO dead. And every time, those who adapted won.

2026 is no different. Only the specific adaptations have changed.

## What's actually changing

### 1. Queries are getting longer and more conversational

AI assistants like Gemini and ChatGPT are training users to search in full sentences and with context. Instead of "SEO agency Amsterdam," someone now searches "which SEO agency is best suited for a B2B SaaS scale-up in Amsterdam looking to generate leads?"

This has two implications. First: long-tail search volumes are growing and becoming more relevant. Second: content that fully answers the question — rather than simply containing the keywords — wins.

### 2. AI Overviews (SGE) reduces clicks on informational queries

Google is increasingly displaying AI-generated summaries above organic results. For simple informational queries ("what is SEO?"), click-through rates can drop significantly.

**But**: for commercial and transactional searches, traditional results remain dominant. People looking for an agency, wanting to buy a product, or seeking a service — they click through.

The implication: shift your focus from top-of-funnel informational content toward middle- and bottom-of-funnel content with higher commercial intent.

### 3. E-E-A-T becomes even more critical

Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness. Google rewards content that demonstrably comes from real experts with real-world experience.

In a world saturated with AI-generated content, authentic expertise is a genuine differentiator. Not just as a ranking factor, but as a reason readers return.

What this means in practice:
- Author pages with credible bios
- Citations and references from external sources
- Real-world examples and case studies
- Regular updates to existing content

### 4. Structured data becomes critical for AI citations

If you want your content cited by AI systems — in Perplexity, ChatGPT Search, or Google AI Overviews — it needs to be machine-readable.

Schema.org structured data helps AI systems understand who you are, what you do, and why you're trustworthy. Sites investing in comprehensive structured data implementation today will have a significant advantage in two years.

## What isn't changing

Despite all the shifts, fundamentals remain:

1. **Great content wins**. Always. Thin, generic content disappears. Deep, opinionated, expert-driven content grows in value.

2. **Authority matters**. Links from trusted sources, media mentions, social proof — all these authority signals remain essential.

3. **Intent is everything**. Content that fully addresses search intent — and already anticipates the next logical question — beats content that merely contains keywords.

## The right strategy for 2026

Our recommendation for organisations that want to grow in the AI era:

1. **Invest in E-E-A-T**. Make expertise visible. Publish with clear authors. Earn external validation.

2. **Shift toward commercial content**. Less how-to, more why-us. Content that supports purchase decisions is less vulnerable to AI Overviews.

3. **Get structured data right**. Implement Schema.org correctly and comprehensively — not just for SEO, but for AI citations too.

4. **Build a loyal audience**. Email subscribers, direct return visitors, branded searches — this group is completely immune to algorithm changes.

5. **Create content AI can't replicate**. Original research, internal data, personal expertise, specific client cases. Unique insights that exist nowhere else.
